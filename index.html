ESPN on windows. Sports goes metro.

A new ESPN visual language, native to Windows.

The worldwide leader in sports tasked POP with developing a library of apps, which blended the visual language of ESPN with what was once called "Metro" - Microsoft's new digital design paradigm.

We set to work creating "Hub" – a Nokia hardware exclusive that brought truly comprehensive sports coverage to any region of the world — and a first for ESPN.

Compared to the brash attitude of SportsCenter, ESPN Hub was designed with a more subtle touch for a world that was not attuned to "da na na". Its colors are softer and more muted then the SportsCenter set. More important, if you're a proper football fan from UK, traveling in India and checking the cricket scores back home, you could jump around in just a few quick swipes. Tne UX was optimized for world sports. All of em.

On the backs of our work with ESPN Hub, we were tasked with an all new platform: Surface. A massive endeaveor, we productized the entire ESPN.com content and feature set into a beautiful, native app which we codenamed... "Ocho". (It made sense at the time... the Metro language first came to Windows 8.)

Kids, take your first step into a larger world.

An all-new kind of Pokémon experience.

I have had the privilege to bring to life countless experiences for an incredible kids brand, Pokémon.

One of these, Camp Pokémon, is a brand new IP my team at POP created to fill a gap in Pokémon’s product ecosystem: invite younger kids on modern devices to learn the ways of the Pokémon universe.

My role was creative director. I guided the ideation, pitched the concept, and architected the experience along with our technical director. We then assembled a team a team of UX/designers, 3D artists and Unity developers to execute.

I’ve directed the redesign of the Pokémon.com website for two digital generations.

The newest iteration (which is live today) was a massive e ort to modernize: build a site that was a hub of amazing content across all brand pillars in a lightweight, responsive web redesign, and infuse it with the spirit of fun that its fans find synonymous with the Pokémon name.

Once upon a time, we introduced Kindle to the world

Three generations of telling the Kindle story.

During the early days of Kindle, e-readers were a brand new concept for the world. It was my job to partner with Amazon's marketing team to tell the world how great the revolution was going to be.

Beyond marketing, I had the pleasure of co-creating the very first edition of Kindle for iPad. A divergence from the previous Amazon mindset to keep brand experiences simple and unadorned, we had a direct challenge from Jeff Bezos: "I want to out-Apple Apple".

The iPad would ship with iBooks and iBooks Store. Our work on the Kindle app established a foundation for one of the most popular iOS apps ever released.

Redefining entertainment for a world of gamers.

The Halo Living Monument.

For the 10th anniversary of the best-selling franchise in Xbox history, we created a monument to fandom, the Halo Living Monument. It stands to this day as a tribute to the story of Xbox and the fans that answered the call.

When a fan uploaded a photo memory and chose their place on the 3D statue, they could watch as their moment was immortalized within the Halo lore. It shone within a dynamic video of a UNSC commander addressing his troops and honoring the fallen.

When we were invited to present our agency POV on the Xbox digital platform, we created this concept: "Pivots". It was bold, authentic and flexible. It answered the question of how an A-list brand should show its own pride for what it is, and tell the story of a new kind of system that truly owned "entertainment".

Xbox One launched with several titles, but no other campaign sold launch titles like our Dead Rising 3 campaign. An integrated concept, we built a choose-your-own-adventure on YouTube out of hundreds of carefully scripted gameplay clips. Fans "played the marketing" to choose their way through the virtual city map and discover for themselves just how massive this next gen zombie mayhem really was.

Kinect Sports Rivals gave us the challenge of showing the world what a next-gen console, equipped with next-gen motion sensors in Kinect, could really do. A virtual tour of a fantasy sports island challenged fans to earn digital rewards as they discovered gameplay tips on the virtual environment.
