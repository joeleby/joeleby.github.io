ESPN on windows. Sports goes metro.

Designing an ESPN visual language on Windows

The worldwide leader in sports tasked POP with developing a library of apps which blended the visual language of ESPN with what was once called "Metro" - Microsoft's design paradigm for its digital ecosystem.

We set to work creating "Hub" – a Nokia hardware exclusive that brought truly comprehensive sports coverage to any region of the world — something which had never been attempted by ESPN at this scale at the time.

ESPN Hub was designed with a more subtle touch for a world that was not familiar with "da na na" (SportsCenter). Its colors are softer and more muted then Chris Berman might have liked. Yet if you're a proper football fan from UK traveling in India and checking the cricket scores back home, you could jump around in just a few quick swipes. UX optimized for world sports.

On the backs of our work with ESPN Hub, we were tasked with an all new platform: Surface. A massive endeaveor, we productized the entire ESPN.com content and feature set into a beautiful, native app which we codenamed "Ocho". (It made sense at the time... the Metro language first came to Windows 8.)
